My journey

From aerospace
to cybersecurity

My story begins in Bangalore, India. After completing a Bachelor of Technology in Aeronautical Engineering at Jain University, I spent three years interning across Asia's most demanding aerospace and engineering organisations — including Sansera Engineering, one of Asia's top-ranked aerospace manufacturers, Brahmastra Aerospace Systems, Pegasus Aerospace, and Autodesk. I designed mechanical components in AutoCAD and SolidWorks, simulated industrial aerodynamics using ANSYS, and worked hands-on with embedded systems and IoT at Probots Techno Solutions.

My final year university project was a fully functioning obstacle-avoidance quadcopter drone. I wrote every line of the Arduino C++ control algorithm myself, designed every component in CAD, integrated four ultrasonic sensors with PID control logic, and flew it live — demonstrating real-time collision avoidance in all four directions. That project taught me that the most important skill in any technical field is not just knowing the tools, but knowing how systems behave under pressure.

In 2023, I moved to Melbourne. I enrolled in the Certificate IV in Cybersecurity at RMIT University and spent a year building a genuine technical foundation — learning SIEM platforms including ELK Stack and QRadar, performing vulnerability assessments, deploying pfSense firewalls and Snort IDS, and completing a full simulated SOC engagement covering all five NIST CSF phases. I used Metasploit, Burp Suite, Wireshark, Cisco Packet Tracer, and Active Directory in real lab environments.

At Medilink Australia I worked as an Operations Administrator managing sprint planning in Jira and Confluence, producing structured stakeholder reports, and supporting compliance documentation in a healthcare-adjacent environment. That role built my understanding of how regulated organisations communicate risk — a skill that transfers directly to SOC case documentation and shift handovers.

Since graduating I have continued building independently — deploying three enterprise SOC home labs, completing cybersecurity programmes with Commonwealth Bank and Mastercard, pursuing TryHackMe's SOC Level 1 pathway, and starting courses in IBM Cybersecurity Architecture and Google AI Essentials. I am currently working at Coles CFC Truganina, with one week notice, actively seeking the right opportunity in cybersecurity, cloud, Azure, project management, or systems administration.
