My journey

From aerospace to cybersecurity

My story begins in Bangalore, India. After completing a Bachelor of Technology in Aeronautical Engineering at Jain University, I spent three years interning across Asia's most demanding aerospace and engineering organisations — including Sansera Engineering, one of Asia's top-ranked aerospace manufacturers, Brahmastra Aerospace Systems, Pegasus Aerospace, and Autodesk. I designed mechanical components in AutoCAD and SolidWorks, simulated industrial aerodynamics using ANSYS, and worked hands-on with embedded systems and IoT at Probots Techno Solutions.

My final year project was a fully functioning obstacle-avoidance quadcopter drone. I wrote every line of the C++ obstacle avoidance algorithm, designed every component in CAD, integrated four ultrasonic sensors with PID control logic, and flew it live — successfully demonstrating real-time collision avoidance in all four directions. That project taught me that the most important skill in any technical field is understanding how systems behave under pressure.

In 2023, I moved to Melbourne and enrolled in the Certificate IV in Cybersecurity at RMIT University — learning SIEM platforms including ELK Stack and QRadar, performing vulnerability assessments, deploying pfSense firewalls and Snort IDS, and completing a full simulated SOC engagement covering all five NIST CSF phases.

At Medilink Australia I worked as an Operations Administrator managing sprint planning via Jira and Confluence, producing structured stakeholder reports, and supporting compliance documentation. I am currently working part-time at Coles CFC Truganina while actively seeking the right opportunity in cybersecurity, cloud, project management, or systems administration.
