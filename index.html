My journey

From Aerospace to Cybersecurity

My story begins in Bangalore, India. After completing a Bachelor of Technology in Aeronautical Engineering at Jain University, I spent three years interning across some of Asia's most demanding aerospace and engineering organisations — including Sansera Engineering, one of Asia's top-ranked aerospace manufacturers, Brahmastra Aerospace Systems, Pegasus Aerospace, and Autodesk. I designed mechanical components in AutoCAD and SolidWorks, simulated industrial aerodynamics using ANSYS, and worked hands-on with embedded systems and IoT at Probots Techno Solutions.

My final year project was a fully functioning obstacle-avoidance quadcopter drone. I wrote every line of the C++ obstacle avoidance algorithm, designed every component in CAD, integrated four ultrasonic sensors with PID control logic, and flew it successfully — demonstrating real-time collision avoidance in all four directions. That project reinforced a principle I carry into cybersecurity: the most important skill is not just knowing the tools, but understanding how systems behave under pressure.

In 2023, I relocated to Melbourne and enrolled in the Certificate IV in Cybersecurity at RMIT University. Over the following year, I built a genuine technical foundation — deploying SIEM platforms including ELK Stack and QRadar, conducting vulnerability assessments, configuring pfSense firewalls and Snort IDS, and completing a full security engagement covering all five NIST CSF phases for a simulated client environment.

At Medilink Australia I served as an Operations Administrator, managing sprint planning in Jira and Confluence, producing structured stakeholder reports, and supporting compliance documentation in a healthcare-adjacent environment. I am currently working part-time at Coles CFC Truganina while actively pursuing opportunities in cybersecurity, cloud, project management, or systems administration.
